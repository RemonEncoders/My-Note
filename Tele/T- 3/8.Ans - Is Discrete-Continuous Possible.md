
# My Question: Is Discrete-Continuous Possible?

Is it possible to have a **discrete-analog** signal? What would that even look like? My understanding makes it hard to visualize something with discrete x-axis values but continuous y-axis values.

Can someone please correct any misunderstandings I have and help me understand whether "discrete-continuous" signals exist and what they might look like?

# ⭐ Step 1: তোমার বুঝাটা কতটা ঠিক?

তুমি বলেছ:

## ✔ Analog vs Digital (Y-axis based)

- Analog = infinite amplitude values
    
- Digital = finite (0/1)
    

## ✔ Continuous vs Discrete (X-axis based)

- Continuous = সব time instant এ value আছে
    
- Discrete = কিছু selected time point এ value আছে
    

👉 এই দুইটা ধারণা **100% correct**

---

# ⭐ Step 2: এখন আসল signal classification

তুমি যে 2D idea দিচ্ছ (x-axis + y-axis), সেটা আসলে perfect।

আমরা এটাকে বলি:

```text
Time axis (x) + Amplitude axis (y)
```

---

# ⭐ Step 3: সব possible combinations

তোমার table আসলে প্রায় correct:

|Time (x)|Amplitude (y)|Type|
|---|---|---|
|Continuous|Continuous|Analog continuous signal|
|Continuous|Discrete|Digital waveform (idealized square wave)|
|Discrete|Continuous|Sampled analog signal|
|Discrete|Discrete|Fully digital signal|

---

# ⭐ Step 4: এখন তোমার main confusion

তুমি জিজ্ঞেস করেছ:

> Discrete-Continuous কি possible?

👉 অর্থাৎ:

- x-axis discrete
    
- y-axis continuous
    

---

# ⭐ Step 5: Answer = YES, এটা exists!

এটাকে বলে:

## ⭐ Sampled Analog Signal

---

# ⭐ Step 6: এটা কীভাবে দেখা যায়?

ধরো:

```text title:"Analog Sampled Signal"
time →
t = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4

signal:
      •
   •  |
   |  | •
   | •| |
   | || | •
   | || | |
  ----------------   
```

এখানে:

## ✔ x-axis (time):

- শুধু নির্দিষ্ট points → DISCRETE
    

## ✔ y-axis (amplitude):

- যেকোনো real value → CONTINUOUS
    

---

# ⭐ Step 7: Real-world example

## 🎤 Audio sampling

Microphone signal:

- original = continuous analog
    
- ADC (Analog to Digital Converter) samples it
    

---

### ADC step 1: sampling

Time select করে:

- 0 ms
    
- 1 ms
    
- 2 ms
    

---

### ADC step 2: before quantization

At each sample:

- value still analog (continuous amplitude)
    

👉 এটা exactly:

## ⭐ DISCRETE-TIME + CONTINUOUS-AMPLITUDE

---

# ⭐ Step 8: তাহলে Digital signal কখন হয়?

Digital হওয়ার জন্য extra step লাগে:

## Quantization

```text
continuous amplitude → rounded levels (0,1,2,...)
```

---

# ⭐ Step 9: Final mapping (very important)

```text
	Analog signal
			|
			|(sampling)
		    ↓ 
Discrete-time & continuous-amplitude
			|
			|(quantization)
		    ↓ 
Discrete-time & discrete-amplitude (Digital signal)

```

---

# ⭐ Step 10: তোমার confusion fix

তুমি বলেছিলে:

> “continuous-digital = square wave”

❌ এটা একটু wrong classification

✔ Square wave:

- time continuous
    
- amplitude discrete (0/1)
    

👉 এটা actually:

## Continuous-time + discrete-amplitude

---

# ⭐ Step 11: corrected full table

|Type|Meaning|Example|
|---|---|---|
|Continuous + Continuous|Analog signal|sine wave|
|Continuous + Discrete|Ideal digital waveform|square wave|
|Discrete + Continuous|Sampled analog signal|ADC output (before quantization)|
|Discrete + Discrete|Digital signal|binary data (0/1 samples)|

---

# ⭐ Step 12: সবচেয়ে গুরুত্বপূর্ণ insight

👉 “Discrete vs Continuous” is NOT about analog/digital directly.

It is about:

## 1. Time axis

## 2. Amplitude axis

They are independent dimensions.

---

# ⭐ Final simple answer

👉 Yes, **Discrete–Continuous signal exists**  
👉 It is called **sampled analog signal**  
👉 It appears in real systems before quantization in ADC

---

# ⭐ One-line memory trick

```text
Sampling = time discrete
Quantization = amplitude discrete
```
