# Continuous Signal vs Discrete Signal

| Feature             | Continuous Signal             | Discrete Signal                                                                              |
| ------------------- | ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Nature              | Exists at every time instant  | Exists only at specific time points                                                          |
| Values              | Continuous values<br>(-∞, +∞) | Separate individual values(Certain Set of Values)<br>But , it can be (-4,-3,-2,-1,0,1,2,3,4) |
| Time                | Continuous time               | Discrete time                                                                                |
| Shape               | Smooth curve                  | Dots / sampled points                                                                        |
| Real-world relation | Natural signals               | Computer/sampled signals                                                                     |
| Example             | Human voice, temperature      | Digital audio samples                                                                        |

---

# 1. Continuous Signal

A continuous signal exists continuously over time.

Meaning:

- signal never “breaks”
    
- every tiny moment has a value
    

---

## Example — Human Voice 🎤

Your voice changes smoothly.

```text
Time → →

      /¯¯\      /¯¯\
_____/    \____/    \____
```

At every time:

- signal exists
    
- value exists
    

---

# 2. Discrete Signal

A discrete signal exists only at certain time points.

Meaning:

- values are taken at intervals
    
- not available at every instant
    

---

## Example — Digital Sampling

Computer records sound like:

```text
•     •     •     •     •
```

Only sampled points are stored.

---

# Real Life Analogy ⏰

Flowing water from a tap 🚰

Smooth nonstop flow.

---

## Discrete

Water drops 💧💧💧

Separate intervals.

# Important Concept

## Continuous Signal

Time is continuous.

```text
t = all possible times
```

---

## Discrete Signal

Time is sampled.

```text
t = 0,1,2,3...
```

---

# Continuous vs Analog

Many students confuse these.

---

## Continuous Signal

Talks about time continuity.

---

## Analog Signal

Talks about amplitude continuity.

Usually analog signals are continuous,  
but conceptually they are different ideas.

---

# Discrete vs Digital

Also related but not exactly same.

---

## Discrete Signal

Sampled in time.

---

## Digital Signal

Sampled + quantized into binary levels.

So:

```text
Digital Signal
    =
Discrete Time
    +
Discrete Amplitude
```

---

# Easy Memory Trick

```text
Continuous = Smooth in time
Discrete   = Step/sample in time
```

# One Line Expert Definition

## Continuous Signal

A signal defined for every instant of time.

## Discrete Signal

A signal defined only at specific time instants.
